# FleshNote Sprint Modes

FleshNote IDE features specialized "Focus Sprint Modes" designed to help writers overcome writer's block, stay focused, and hit their word count goals through gamification and unique constraints.

## Available Modes

### 1. Kamikaze Mode
**High-Stakes Writing.** If you stop typing for too long, your text begins to turn red and will eventually be deleted! You must keep the words flowing to survive the sprint.
- **Best for:** Forcing out a first draft, overcoming severe perfectionism.

### 2. Fog Mode
**Anti-Distraction.** Older text gradually fades into a "fog," keeping only your most recent sentences clearly visible. 
- **Best for:** Writers who get caught up re-reading and endlessly tweaking previous paragraphs instead of moving forward.

### 3. Hemingway Mode
**Forward Only.** The backspace and delete keys are disabled. You cannot edit what you've written, you can only write the next word.
- **Best for:** Silencing your inner editor and focusing purely on getting ideas onto the page.

### 4. Combo Mode
**Gamified Speed.** typing consistently builds up a "combo meter." The faster you type without stopping, the higher your combo goes, complete with visual feedback.
- **Best for:** Motivating yourself to type faster and maintain a steady flow state.

### 5. Momentum Mode
**Pacing.** Focuses on keeping a steady, continuous rhythm of output.

### 6. Zen Mode
**Relaxing Growth.** As you write and hit your word count goals, procedural plants and scenery grow on the screen. Hitting your goal triggers a blooming effect.
- **Best for:** A calm, rewarding, and visually pleasing writing session.

## Usage
Select a focus mode from the goal setter in the editor toolbar. You can specify a target word count for your sprint, and FleshNote will keep track of your progress. You cannot exit the mode until the word count is reached!
